Service, Leadership, DEI

Working with a diverse set of collaborators has confirmed my belief that anyone has the potential to become a great computer scientist, and that there is great strength in bringing together people from different backgrounds and cultures or with different identities. For this reason, it is crucial to dismantle barriers to diversity in computer science (e.g., lack of role models, stereotypical culture), which I have made a priority during my graduate studies at the University of Washington's (UW) Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering. As a professor, I plan to continue my efforts to increase diversity and belonging in computer science in three different directions, drawing on my past experience: - Broadening Participation Through Advising and Outreach – mentorship and outreach to support and recruit women, students of color, and LGBTQ+ students - Positive Societal Impact Through Research – research and workshop organization to promote fairness, justice, and ethics in artificial intelligence - Supportive and Inclusive Environment Through Structural Change – departmental service, research community leadership, and community organization In all my efforts, I strive to use evidence-based practices and center experiences from historically underrepresented groups (including, when relevant, my own as an out LGBTQ+ person). For more detailed information, please see my [diversity statement](files/2020jobsearch/maarten_diversity_statement.pdf).
